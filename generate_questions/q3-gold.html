ITALY

  • A VEN S A TYR - TRI
  • A TYR - TRI
  • A TRI - ALB
  • A ROM - NAP
Suggestion Explanation
This move set is focused on regaining control of key strategic spaces—Trieste and the Ionian Sea—before France and Turkey fully encircle Italy’s southern flank and Balkan access. Retaking Trieste with TYR + VEN ensures Austria’s old holdings don't become an easy Turkish springboard, while F ADR - ION contests French naval dominance and prevents a full lock on the central Mediterranean. Moving TRI - ALB offers tactical flexibility: either as a decoy enabling the supported attack on Trieste or as pressure against Turkish Balkan lines. ROM - NAP secures the Italian boot’s rear, preventing opportunistic strikes on home centers while freeing Rome for future mobilization. This combination halts foreign momentum and reestablishes Italy as a power with initiative in the south.
RUSSIA

Even if unsuccessful, it destabilizes central Europe and opens room for negotiation or survival. In Russia's fragile state, forcing chaos on stronger powers is the only path to relevance.
